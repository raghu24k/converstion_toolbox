# Product Design Documentation

## Problem Definition
In today's digital world, working with files is often frustrating and inefficient. Users face several key problems when trying to manage their documents and images:

1.  **Too Many Websites**: To perform different tasks—like converting a PDF, merging documents, or removing an image background—users currently have to visit three or four different websites. This is time-consuming and annoying.
2.  **Bad User Experience**: Most free online tools are cluttered with aggressive advertisements, confusing download buttons, and outdated designs that are hard to navigate.
3.  **Privacy Concerns**: Users are often afraid to upload personal or sensitive documents to unknown websites because they don't know if their data is being stored or shared.
4.  **High Costs**: Professional software like Adobe Acrobat or Photoshop provides these features but is very expensive. Casual users shouldn't have to pay a monthly subscription just to convert a single file.

## Concept
**ToolBox** is designed to be the ultimate free digital utility belt. The core concept is built around three pillars:

1.  **Centralization (All-in-One)**: 
    Instead of bookmarking ten different sites, users have one reliable destination. ToolBox brings together the most essential PDF and image tools (Converter, Merger, Cropper, Background Remover) into a single, unified dashboard.

2.  **Premium Simplicity**: 
    We believe free tools shouldn't look "cheap." ToolBox uses a modern, clean, and ad-free interface that puts the user's task first. It is designed to be intuitive—if you can click a button, you can use this tool.

3.  **Privacy & Speed**: 
    The platform is built to be fast and secure. It requires no login or registration. Files are processed securely and are automatically deleted from the server immediately after the work is done, ensuring complete user privacy.
